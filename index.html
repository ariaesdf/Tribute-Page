Ham "Lustboy" Jang-sik

From high level StarCraft player to League of Legends World Competitor

Lustboy after a regular season victory on Team Solo Mid in 2014

A Young Beginning

Ham Jang-sik started playing video games at just 7 years old with Starcraft: Brood Wars. He began playing well after the game was competitively popular. Unfortunately for Lustboy this meant there wasn't much room for innovation. In his own words "I really like to make innovation...I like trying new things no one's ever tried before. But [Blizzard] already made SC1 a long time ago so there [wasn't much for me to do]." He later played Starcraft II at the Semi-Pro level joining Korean Team Prime as a substitute in 2010. The following year he began playing League of Legends.

Lustboy's League of Legends Career Timeline

  • 2011 - MiG Blaze. Brought on by AD Carry player Helio, Lustboy and MiG Blaze took first place at the first OnGamingNet tournament at Champions Spring 2012
  • 2013 - MiG Blaze is acquired by CJ Entus and renamed CJ Entus Blaze. The team continued to place first in the World Cyber Games Korean qualifiers.
  • 2014 -Lustboy leaves South Korea and CJ Entus Blaze to join Team Solo Mid in North America. After finishing as the top team in the league Lustboy retired from his position as a player, and moved to a coaching role.
  • 2015 -Present - Lustboy continues coaching and analyzing for professional League of Legends teams, currently for 100Thieves, and has lead teams to regional playoffs victories and even the World Championship Tournament.
